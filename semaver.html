sem(a)ver

To understand this, you must know semver.

 

Moving on, sem(a)ver adds a few (well, one) change(s).

  • a ma tag and a

What is this?

ma(major) is for a major change. Use it for api rewrites (having the same commands, so being backwards compatible) or a major improvement, that is STILL backwards compatible.

READ ON: Do NOT use ma for a MAJOR CHANGE. ma, while being shorthand for MAJOR, is merely a human indicator. It shows "stuff has changed! Check out the changelog! you'll love it and most likely like the new stuff!"

 

Key points to remember:

  1. This tags are optional.

    • Only use them when you feel a release deserves extra attention.

    • Not every release needs ma.

  2. They are human signals, not version rules.

    • The numeric version (X.Y.Z) still follows normal SemVer.

    • ma and mi just tell users: “Hey, pay attention!”

  3. Patch releases remain the same.

    • Bug fixes or very minor improvements don’t need a tag unless you think it’s noteworthy.

  4. Placement:

    • Add the tag at the end of the version string:

      • Example: 1.4.0-ma → big, backward-compatible rewrite

  5. Release notes are your friend.

    • Even with ma, explain what changed in the changelog so users know why it’s tagged.
